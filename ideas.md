<idea>
**Design Movement:** High-Fashion Editorial — inspired by Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, and Allure magazine aesthetics

**Core Principles:**
1. Bold typography dominates — oversized headlines command attention before images
2. High-contrast black and white with a single vibrant accent (deep crimson or hot coral)
3. Asymmetric editorial grid layouts — content breaks the grid intentionally
4. Photography-forward: product images treated as editorial spreads, not catalog shots

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: Warm off-white (#FAF8F5) — feels like premium magazine paper
- Primary text: Near-black (#1A1A1A)
- Accent: Deep Crimson (#C41E3A) — bold, luxurious, feminine authority
- Secondary accent: Warm Gold (#C9A84C) — for ratings, highlights, CTAs
- Muted: Warm gray (#8C8C8C)

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Full-bleed hero sections with text overlaid on dramatic product photography
- Asymmetric two-column layouts where text and image weights shift
- Horizontal scrolling "featured" strips for product carousels
- Category pages use a magazine-spread masonry grid
- Pull quotes and callout boxes break the reading flow intentionally

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thick editorial rule lines (2-4px) separating sections — a classic magazine divider
2. Oversized category labels in all-caps serif font positioned vertically along the left margin
3. "Best Pick" badges styled like wax seals or magazine award stamps

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover on product cards reveals a second image (model using the product)
- Smooth page transitions with a brief fade-through-white effect
- Sticky navigation that compresses to a slim bar on scroll

**Animation:**
- Headline text slides up on page load (staggered, 0.1s delay per word)
- Product cards have a subtle lift + shadow deepen on hover (transform: translateY(-4px))
- Star ratings animate in with a left-to-right fill on first viewport entry
- "Buy on Amazon" button pulses once on hover to draw attention

**Typography System:**
- Display/Headlines: Playfair Display (serif) — bold, authoritative, feminine
- Body: Source Serif 4 — readable, editorial feel
- Labels/UI: DM Sans — clean, modern contrast to the serif
- Hierarchy: 72px hero → 48px section → 32px card → 18px body → 14px meta

## SELECTED DESIGN: Option 3 — Refined Magazine Meets Bold Lifestyle

**Rationale:** This approach best serves the dual goals of "magazine style" and "bold/vibrant" as requested. It has editorial authority through the serif display font and structured layouts, while the warm burgundy + amber palette delivers the boldness and vibrancy needed to stand out. The cream background feels premium without being cold, and the blush rose accent keeps it feminine and on-brand for a women's hair care site.
